AI Builder Lab

Beyond ChatGPT: help your teen become a responsible builder with AI.

We're a homeschool family asking the same question many parents are asking right now: how do we prepare our kids for a future where the jobs, tools, and creative opportunities may look nothing like the past?

AI Builder Lab is a hands-on program for self-directed teens and their parents. We explore AI as a thinking partner, creative studio, research assistant, coding collaborator, and prototyping tool — without losing human judgment, curiosity, or voice.

Parents can feel the shift. The old map is incomplete.

College may still matter. Traditional skills still matter. But many families can feel that the old "study hard, pick a major, get a stable career" story no longer answers the whole question.

AI is already changing how people write, research, design, code, automate, make media, start businesses, and learn new skills. At the same time, parents are right to worry about screen time, safety, misinformation, cheating, dependency, and low-quality AI slop.

The answer is not to ignore AI. And it is not to hand kids a chatbot and hope for the best.

The answer is guided, responsible builder fluency.

The reframe

AI should be a partner, not a replacement.

We teach teens to use AI without outsourcing their mind.

That means learning how to ask better questions, test ideas, check facts, build prototypes, express themselves, and decide when not to use AI at all.

Thinking Partner

Use AI to explore questions, generate possibilities, and sharpen ideas — not replace original thought.

Creative Studio

Turn interests into storyboards, visuals, scripts, videos, presentations, and creative experiments.

Research Assistant

Learn how to investigate, compare sources, ask follow-up questions, and avoid confident nonsense.

Builder Partner

Move beyond chat into prototypes, websites, automations, agents, and simple software-assisted workflows.

The path

A simple path from curiosity to demo

Week 1

AI as Thinking Partner

Students choose an interest, question, or problem and learn how to turn it into a project direction using AI as a brainstorming and planning partner.

Output → Personal Builder Map
Week 2

AI as Creative Studio

Students explore creative tools for images, video, storytelling, design, and presentation. The focus is on expression, not slop.

Output → Visual concept, storyboard, or media artifact
Week 3

AI as Builder Partner

Students move beyond chat into simple prototypes, coding assistants, workflow tools, automations, and agent-like systems.

Output → Prototype, workflow, mini-site, or build plan
Week 4

Demo Day

Students refine and present what they built. Parents see the work, the process, and the new capabilities their teen is developing.

Output → Demo Day presentation

Where it leads

Demo Day turns learning into community.

The cohort ends with a small Demo Day where students share what they built, what they learned, and what they want to explore next.

This is not about polished perfection. It is about agency, confidence, and showing families what becomes possible when teens learn to use powerful tools responsibly.

Our hope is that Demo Day becomes the beginning of a local builder community for homeschool and worldschool families in the South Bay.

Why we're building this

We're building AI Builder Lab because we're asking these questions in our own family.

We want our children to be prepared for a world that is changing quickly — but not at the cost of their creativity, judgment, relationships, or sense of self.

We also know many homeschool and worldschool families are trying to solve the same puzzle. How do we prepare our kids for uncertain future work? How do we help them use powerful tools safely? How do we build community around learning instead of leaving every family to figure it out alone?

Questions parents ask

Is this a coding class?+

No. Coding may be part of what we explore, but this is broader than coding. AI Builder Lab is about helping teens become capable builders: people who can think, create, research, prototype, and present using modern AI tools.

Is this just ChatGPT?+

No. ChatGPT and Claude may be starting points, but the course goes beyond chat into creative tools, video models, coding assistants, automation workflows, and agent-like systems.

Is AI safe for kids?+

Parents are right to ask this. We do not treat AI as a toy or babysitter. The program emphasizes parent awareness, age-appropriate use, safety boundaries, fact-checking, authorship, and responsible creative practice.

Do parents attend?+

The free workshop is for parents. The 4-week cohort is designed for teens 13+, with parent involvement during onboarding and Demo Day. We may also offer a parent-focused cohort later if there is interest.

What age is this for?+

The first cohort is designed for teens 13+. If you have younger children, you can still join the interest list because we may create younger family workshops later.

Where will it happen?+

We are currently gathering interest from South Bay families and exploring local venue options. The first cohort will be local to the South Bay if enough families sign up.

How much is the founding cohort?+

The founding cohort price is $500. It includes four weekly sessions, a 1:1 onboarding conversation, and Demo Day.

What will my teen make?+

Each student will choose a project direction based on their interests. Possible outputs include a visual concept, presentation, story, video prototype, mini-site, automation idea, research project, or simple software-assisted build.

Is this an accredited school program?+

No. AI Builder Lab is an enrichment workshop and project-based learning cohort. It is not an accredited school and does not replace your family's homeschool, charter, or independent study requirements.
